Study Notes — What We Did, Simply
1. Load Data

Loaded the Excel file into Python using pandas

2. Ratio Averages

Used groupby('Company') to get each company's 5-year average for all 6 ratios

3. Composite Score (Ranking)

Ratios were on different scales (ROE 15-47%, Debt-Equity 0.08-0.24), so we first normalized everything to a 0-100 scale
Formula: (value - min)/(max - min) × 100
Reversed the formula for Debt-Equity (lower debt = better score)
Used equal weights (16.67% each, for 6 ratios) to avoid bias
Added everything up, averaged per company, and got the final ranking — TCS came #1

4. Prediction

Used LinearRegression() for each company to fit a trend line on 5 years of data
Used model.predict([[2027]]) to estimate next year's profit
Checked R² score to see how reliable each prediction was — TCS/Infosys strong, Wipro/Tech Mahindra weak

5. Clustering

Used StandardScaler() to bring all ratios to the same scale
Used KMeans(n_clusters=3) to automatically split companies into 3 groups
Labeled the group with the highest ROE as "High Performer"

6. Charts

Used matplotlib to create 3 charts (Ranking, Prediction, Clusters) side by side
